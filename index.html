Hypatia of Alexandria

by Ciara - Class 5H

This interesting looking lady 👆is Hypatia of Alexandria (born c. 350–370; died 415). I decided to research her because she was a remarkable woman practicing science nearly 1,700 years ago. At that time most people involved in science were men, and women were discouraged from becoming educated 👎.

Hypatia was born in Alexandria, Egypt, which was in the Roman Empire.

Hypatia was well known for being a philosopher, astronomer, mathematician, and teacher. Students and other teachers came from all over to hear her speak. 👩‍🎓

She refused to wear traditional womens clothes and wore scholars robes like the male teachers! You can see her in the robes in this painting by Robert Trewick Bone 👇

She studied in The Great Library of Alexandria and she even wrote her own scrolls 📜. Sadly, none of her work has survived until today as it was destroyed when a fire burned down the library 🔥🏛🔥😱.

She got murdered by a Christian mob in 415 AD. Her limbs got dragged to a town called Cinarion and they got burnt 😢. Here is a illustration of her death by Louis Figuier 👇.
